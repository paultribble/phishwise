## Welcome

PhishWise is a phishing awareness training platform designed to help you recognize and respond to phishing emails. You will receive simulated phishing emails at randomized intervals. If you click a link in one of these simulations, you will be redirected to a short training module that teaches you about the phishing technique used.

The goal is not to trick you -- it is to build your ability to spot phishing attempts before they cause real harm.

## Getting Started

### Creating an Account

1. Visit the PhishWise home page
2. Click **Sign Up**
3. Enter your name, email address, and a password (minimum 8 characters)
4. Click **Create Account**
5. You will be redirected to the login page

### Signing In

PhishWise supports three sign-in methods:

**Email + Password:**
1. Go to the login page
2. Enter your email and password
3. Click **Sign In**

**Magic Link (Passwordless):**
1. Go to the login page
2. Click **Sign in with Magic Link**
3. Enter your email address
4. Check your inbox for a sign-in link (valid for 30 minutes)
5. Click the link to sign in automatically

**Forgot Password:**
1. Click **Forgot Password?** on the login page
2. Enter your email address
3. Check your inbox for a reset link (valid for 1 hour)
4. Click the link and set a new password

### First-Time Setup

After signing in for the first time:

1. You will see a prompt to join a school or create one
2. If your organization has given you an **invite code**, enter it to join their school
3. If you are setting up a new organization, click **Create School** (you will become a Manager)

## Your Dashboard

After signing in, you will see your personal dashboard with the following sections:

### Stats Overview

Four key metrics are displayed at the top:

- **Total Simulations** -- Number of phishing simulation emails sent to you
- **Simulations Clicked** -- How many you clicked (lower is better)
- **Training Completed** -- Number of training modules you have finished
- **Click Rate** -- Percentage of simulations you clicked (goal: 0%)

### Pending Training

If you have training modules that need to be completed (either from clicking a simulation or assigned by your manager), they appear as a banner at the top of your dashboard. Click a module name to start it.

### Simulation History

A table showing all simulations sent to you, including:

- **Date** -- When the simulation was sent
- **Template** -- The phishing email template used
- **Status** -- Whether you clicked the link
- **Training** -- Whether you completed the associated training module

## Receiving Phishing Simulations

### What to Expect

You will receive simulated phishing emails in your real inbox. These emails are designed to look like genuine phishing attempts -- they may impersonate well-known companies like Amazon, Microsoft, PayPal, or UPS.

The emails will:
- Come from realistic-looking sender addresses
- Contain urgent language prompting you to take action
- Include a link that appears to go to a legitimate website

### If You Click the Link

1. The click is recorded in your simulation history
2. You are redirected to a **"You Got Caught"** page that explains the red flags
3. You are then taken to a training module about the phishing technique
4. Complete the training quiz to mark the module as done
5. Your click rate increases (this is what you want to avoid)

### If You Recognize It as Phishing

Excellent work. The simulation will be recorded as "not clicked" in your history, and your click rate stays low. Over time, you should see your click rate decrease as you get better at spotting these attempts.

### Simulation Frequency

Your school manager controls how often simulations are sent. Common schedules include:

- **Weekly** -- One simulation per week
- **Biweekly** -- One every two weeks
- **Monthly** -- One per month
- **Daily** -- One per day (for intensive training periods)

You can override this in your Settings (see below).

## Training Modules

### How Training Works

Training modules teach you about specific phishing techniques. Each module includes:

- An explanation of the phishing technique
- Examples of real-world attacks using this method
- Red flags to look for
- A quiz to test your understanding

### Available Topics

PhishWise includes training modules covering:

- **Credential Harvesting** -- Fake login pages that steal your password
- **Business Email Compromise (BEC)** -- Impersonation of executives or vendors
- **CEO Fraud** -- Requests from fake leadership for urgent wire transfers
- **Malware Distribution** -- Attachments or links that install malware
- **Spear Phishing** -- Targeted attacks using personal information
- **Package Delivery Scams** -- Fake shipping notifications
- **Account Verification** -- Fake security alerts asking you to "verify" your account
- **Payment Processing** -- Fake invoices or payment confirmations

### Completing a Module

1. Click the module name from your dashboard or the training page
2. Read through the educational content
3. Review the red flags and examples
4. Complete the quiz at the end
5. Your score is recorded and the module is marked as complete

### Viewing All Modules

Visit the **Training** page from the navigation bar to see all available modules, whether they are assigned to you, and your completion status.

## The "You Got Caught" Page

When you click a link in a phishing simulation, you are taken to a page that shows:

- **The email subject** that tricked you
- **The sender address** and why it was suspicious
- **Red flags** specific to that email, such as:
  - Urgent language pressuring immediate action
  - Sender domain that does not match the company
  - Generic greeting instead of your name
  - Suspicious URL that does not match the legitimate website

This page is designed to be educational, not punitive. Take a moment to review the red flags before proceeding to the full training module.

## Joining a School

A "school" in PhishWise is an organization or group managed by one or more administrators. Schools allow managers to send simulations, track performance, and assign training.

### With an Invite Code

1. Sign in to PhishWise
2. On your dashboard, look for **Join a School**
3. Enter the invite code provided by your manager (e.g., `A1B2C3D4`)
4. Click **Join**
5. You are now part of the school and will begin receiving simulations

### Via Email Invitation

1. Check your email for an invitation from your manager
2. The email contains your school's invite code and a sign-up link
3. Click the link or copy the invite code
4. Sign in and enter the code

## Settings and Preferences

Access your settings from the navigation bar by clicking **Settings**.

### Update Your Name

1. Go to Settings
2. Edit the **Name** field
3. Click **Save Changes**

### Change Your Password

1. Go to Settings
2. Enter your **Current Password**
3. Enter your **New Password** (minimum 8 characters)
4. Click **Update Password**

Note: If you signed up via magic link and never set a password, you will need to use the "Forgot Password" flow first.

### Simulation Frequency Override

You can override your school's default simulation frequency:

- **Use school default** -- Receive simulations at your school's schedule
- **Weekly** -- Receive one per week regardless of school setting
- **Random** -- Receive at random intervals
- **Off** -- Temporarily stop receiving simulations

To change:
1. Go to Settings
2. Select your preferred frequency
3. Click **Save Changes**

### Pause/Resume Simulations

If you need to stop receiving simulations temporarily:

1. Go to Settings
2. Toggle **Pause Simulations**
3. When ready, toggle it back to resume

You can also unsubscribe via the link included in simulation emails. This sets your account to "paused" and can be reversed in Settings.

## Understanding Your Stats

### Click Rate

Your click rate is the percentage of simulations you clicked:

```
Click Rate = (Simulations Clicked / Total Simulations) x 100
```

- **0-10%** -- Excellent awareness
- **10-20%** -- Good, but room for improvement
- **20-30%** -- Average (industry benchmark)
- **30%+** -- Needs attention (your manager may assign additional training)

### Training Completion

The number of training modules you have completed out of those assigned to you. Aim for 100% completion.

### Trends

Over time, your click rate should decrease as you complete more training and become better at recognizing phishing attempts. Your manager can see your trend data in their analytics dashboard.

## Tips for Spotting Phishing

Use these techniques to identify phishing emails before clicking:

### Check the Sender

- **Hover over the sender name** to see the actual email address
- Look for slight misspellings: `amaz0n.com`, `paypa1.com`, `microsft.com`
- Legitimate companies use their official domain, not free email services

### Inspect Links Before Clicking

- **Hover over links** to see the actual URL in your browser's status bar
- Look for domain mismatches: a link claiming to go to `amazon.com` but actually pointing to `amazon-verify-login.com`
- Be suspicious of shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl) in professional emails

### Watch for Urgency

- "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours"
- "Immediate action required"
- "Confirm your identity now or lose access"
- Legitimate companies rarely demand urgent action via email

### Look for Generic Greetings

- "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name
- "Dear User" or "Dear Account Holder"
- Legitimate companies usually address you by name

### Verify Independently

- If an email claims to be from your bank, go directly to your bank's website (type the URL manually)
- Call the company using a phone number from their official website, not from the email
- Do not use contact information provided in the suspicious email

### Trust Your Instincts

- If something feels off, it probably is
- When in doubt, do not click -- verify through another channel
- It is always better to be cautious than to fall for a phishing attempt

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is PhishWise sending me real phishing emails?**
A: No. PhishWise simulations are controlled, tracked, and harmless. The links in simulation emails go to PhishWise training pages, not malicious websites. No data is stolen and no malware is installed.

**Q: Will I get in trouble if I click a simulation?**
A: No. PhishWise is a training tool, not a punitive system. Clicking a simulation means you get additional training, which helps you improve. Your manager can see your click rate, but the goal is improvement, not punishment.

**Q: How often will I receive simulations?**
A: This depends on your school's settings. Typical frequencies are weekly, biweekly, or monthly. You can see and override this in your Settings.

**Q: Can I stop receiving simulations?**
A: Yes. Go to Settings and select "Off" for your frequency override, or use the unsubscribe link in simulation emails. You can resume at any time.

**Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?**
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page. Enter your email and check your inbox for a reset link. Alternatively, use the Magic Link sign-in option.

**Q: Who can see my results?**
A: Your school manager can see your click rate, training completion, and simulation history. Platform administrators can see aggregate data across all schools. Your specific results are not shared publicly.

**Q: How do I know if an email is a real phishing attempt vs. a PhishWise simulation?**
A: PhishWise simulation links always redirect to the PhishWise training platform. Real phishing emails may lead to credential-stealing pages or malware downloads. If you receive a suspicious email and are unsure, do not click any links -- report it to your IT department.

**Q: Can I take training modules without clicking a simulation?**
A: Yes. Visit the Training page from the navigation bar to browse all available modules. Your manager can also assign specific modules to you directly.

**Q: What browser should I use?**
A: PhishWise works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. Mobile browsers are also supported with a responsive layout.
