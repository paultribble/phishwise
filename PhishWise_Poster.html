Introduction

PhishWise is a web-based phishing awareness and training system designed to help individuals and households recognize and avoid online scams. The platform sends simulated phishing emails based on common scam patterns, then redirects users to short educational modules when they interact with them.

By focusing on individuals, families, and small groups rather than large organizations, PhishWise provides a more personal and accessible training experience than corporate-scale alternatives like KnowBe4 or Sophos.

Problem Statement

Phishing-related financial damages increased 74% from 2020 to 2021, reaching $1.7 billion. The FBI's IC3 reported individuals 60+ filed 150,000 cybercrime complaints in 2024, losing nearly $5 billion — more than any other age group.

Existing training solutions are not accessible to everyday users:

  • KnowBe4 — $3.25/person minimum, groups of 25–50 only
  • Microsoft Defender — enterprise Plan 2 subscription only
  • PhishingBox — free tier: 10 static questions, no real email simulation
  • CanIPhish — requires administrator setup and campaign configuration

PhishWise addresses this gap with automated, affordable, realistic training for anyone.

Training Curriculum

Eight CISA and NIST-aligned training modules, each following a structured 7-section format:

  • Overview — attack type and common usage
  • Social Engineering Tactics — psychological manipulation methods
  • Red Flags — warning signs per CISA/NIST standards
  • Attack Objective — what attackers are after
  • Examples — annotated real-world scenarios
  • Prevention Steps — actionable safer-email habits
  • Practice Check — required quiz if user failed a simulation

20 phishing email templates (difficulty 1–5) built with MJML for consistent rendering across email clients. Each uses display-name spoofing and dynamic reply-to fields to mimic real attacks.

Performance & Security
Sub-100ms click tracking — tracking tokens indexed for O(1) lookup; redirect to training module issued in under 100 milliseconds
Security headers — CSP, HSTS (production), X-Frame-Options, Referrer-Policy, Permissions-Policy via Next.js middleware
15 standardized error codes — ERR_UNAUTHORIZED, ERR_INVALID_INVITE, ERR_RATE_LIMIT, and 12 more across all routes
Structured JSON logging — context-aware (route, userId, error); no sensitive data ever logged
User Roles & Capabilities
USER Receives randomized phishing simulations via email. Upon clicking a simulated link, is immediately redirected to the linked training module. Accesses a personal dashboard for simulation history, performance metrics, and an awareness score. May self-report suspected phishing with "I Caught This."
MANAGER Creates or administers a "school" (group). Sets simulation frequency (weekly / random / custom). Views an expanded analytics dashboard for all members — click rates, training completion, users at risk. Invites members via secure email join codes and can assign supplemental training modules.
ADMIN Maintains platform-wide infrastructure: manages the phishing template library and training module content, oversees the simulation scheduler, and accesses anonymized platform-wide performance data to evaluate trends and improve training effectiveness.
